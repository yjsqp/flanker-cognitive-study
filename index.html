Research Question

Does brief exposure to rapidly changing stimuli impair executive control and decision accuracy, and are these effects temporary?

Hypothesis

Brief exposure to rapidly changing stimuli will temporarily impair executive control, resulting in slower response times and reduced accuracy on a decision-making task that requires resolving conflicting information. After a short recovery period without stimulation, executive control performance will improve toward baseline levels.

Study Design

This study used a within-subject experimental design, in which each participant completed the same cognitive task under three conditions: baseline, after exposure to rapidly changing stimuli, and after a recovery period. Using the same participants across all conditions reduced individual variability and allowed for direct comparison of performance changes.
